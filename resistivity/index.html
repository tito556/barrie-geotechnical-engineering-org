When we deploy the ABEM Terrameter on a site in Barrie, the first thing we check is ground coupling across the electrode array. The resistivity method pushes DC current through steel stakes spaced along a spread, and the potential drop between inner electrodes gives us a direct reading of subsurface material properties. In the Kempenfelt Bay area, the glacial sediments deposited by the retreat of Lake Algonquin create layered sequences of silty clay, sand, and till, each with a distinct electrical signature. We run both VES soundings and 2D resistivity profiles depending on target depth. The equipment fits into a pickup truck and sets up in under 30 minutes on most residential lots. For deeper bedrock mapping near the Oro Moraine, we extend cable spreads to 200 meters or more to reach the limestone and shale contact. The data feeds directly into our site characterization workflow, complementing CPT testing when we need continuous stratigraphic profiles with mechanical tip resistance, or MASW surveys for Vs30 site classification in the same mobilisation day.

Electrical resistivity gives us a non-invasive window into Barrie's glacial stratigraphy before we break ground.

Process overview

The south-end subdivisions near Mapleview Drive sit on thick clay plains with resistivity values often reading below 20 ohm-m, while the drumlin fields north of Highway 400 show compacted till that pushes readings past 80 ohm-m. That contrast is what makes resistivity a fast screening tool before we commit to intrusive work. A VES sounding curve from a backyard in the Holly neighbourhood will typically show a three-layer model: conductive topsoil, a resistive sand lens from the old shoreline deposits, and the drop back to low resistivity as you hit saturated clay. We invert the data using least-squares routines to build a layered earth model that ties directly to borehole logs. When the target is groundwater table depth, the resistivity method is often faster than test pitting, especially in winter when the ground is frozen to half a meter and mechanical excavation slows down. The electrode geometry and spacing follow ASTM D6431, and we document every sounding with GPS coordinates, contact resistance checks, and raw apparent resistivity curves for QA/QC traceability.

Local context

We were called to a commercial site on Anne Street where the owner had a geotechnical report from 1998 that showed competent till at 4 meters. The excavator hit flowing sand at 2.5 meters, and the trench collapsed. A quick resistivity profile across the building footprint revealed a buried channel of coarse outwash sand with resistivity values around 150 ohm-m cutting diagonally across the lot. The old borehole had missed it by 15 meters. In Barrie, the interface between the Kempenfelt lacustrine deposits and the Simcoe lobe till is rarely a flat contact. Channels, lenses, and interbedded layers are common. Skipping a geophysical survey on a site with variable Quaternary geology means you are designing based on a point measurement that may not represent the whole footprint. A VES survey combined with SPT drilling at targeted locations gives you the continuous lateral coverage that boreholes alone cannot provide, reducing the chance of encountering unexpected ground conditions during excavation.

Additional services

01

VES Sounding for Depth-to-Bedrock

Schlumberger array soundings with expanding electrode spacing (AB/2 from 1.5 m to 150 m) to map bedrock surface, detect karst features in limestone, and identify overburden thickness. Each sounding includes 1D inversion and correlation with available borehole data.

02

2D Electrical Resistivity Tomography (ERT)

Multi-electrode profiles using 48 or 72 electrodes for continuous cross-sectional imaging. Applied to pipeline route investigations, landfill liner integrity checks, and groundwater plume mapping. Data processed with solid inversion algorithms and presented as colour-contoured resistivity sections.

03

Soil Corrosivity Assessment

Resistivity measurements at multiple depths to classify soil aggressiveness toward buried concrete and steel per CSA and ASTM criteria. Report includes resistivity ranges, pH sampling integration, and recommendations for protective coatings or cathodic protection where required.

Top questions

How does electrical resistivity help in Barrie's glacial soils?

The method distinguishes between clay, silt, sand, and till based on their electrical properties. Conductive clays show low resistivity (5-30 ohm-m), while dry sand and gravel show high resistivity (100-1000 ohm-m). This contrast is particularly useful in Barrie where glaciolacustrine deposits interfinger with till, helping us map lateral changes that affect foundation design.

What does a resistivity survey cost for a typical residential lot in Barrie?

For a standard VES survey on a single-family lot, you are looking at CA$790 to CA$1,500 depending on the number of soundings, electrode spread length, and whether we combine it with 2D profiling. Commercial projects with multiple lines and deeper investigation targets are priced based on linear meters surveyed.

How deep can you investigate with resistivity in this area?

With VES using a Schlumberger array and maximum current electrode spacing of 200 m, we typically reach 50 to 60 meters depth in Barrie's overburden. The practical limit depends on the resistivity contrast between layers and the available space to lay out the cable spread. For deeper targets like bedrock aquifer mapping, we use longer spreads and higher-power transmitters.

Can you run resistivity surveys in winter when the ground is frozen?

Yes, but with some adjustments. Frozen surface soil has very high resistivity, which can mask the signal from deeper layers. We use salt water or bentonite mud around each electrode stake to improve ground coupling, and we apply longer injection times. Winter surveys in Barrie are actually easier for access to agricultural fields and swampy areas that are impassable in spring.
